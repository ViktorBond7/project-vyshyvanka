About Us

The store "Vyshyvanka Vibes" is a place where traditional Ukrainian vyshyvanka comes to life with new zest and style. Our store offers a wide selection of high-quality and authentic embroidery, which combines the elegance of traditional needlework and modern design.

In "Vyshyvanka Vibes" you will find various models of embroidery, from classic to modern, corresponding to the latest fashion trends. Our embroidery is made using natural fabrics and is embroidered by highly qualified craftsmen who convey the beauty of Ukrainian culture in every detail.We believe that vyshyvanka is not just clothing, but a symbol of national pride, the heritage of our ancestors and a way of expressing one's individuality. Each embroidery from "Vyshyvanka Vibes" has its own unique history and symbolism, which gives it a special meaning.

  • In the Yavoriv district, the geometric ornament of the 19th century was replaced by a stylised floral one in the early 20th century.

    Its characteristic features are bright and embossed embroidery, different but balanced small motifs, with a lot of white space between them.

    It is how shirts, headdresses, camisoles and aprons were decorated, creating a light but bright image.

    women's blouse crimea

    4000 UAH

  • The textured embroidery of this shirt reproduces the ornamentation of the Chernihiv region, Nizhyn district, in the first half of the 20th century.

    Various machine embroidery techniques were used to imitate hand stitches.

    In the folk tradition, shirts "embroidered with oak" were common - the embroidery thread was dyed in a decoction of oak bark.

    An interesting technique is the punch needle one, known as "nightingale eyes", when it is used to make the entire element of the ornament.

    Namely: Trident, sign of Volodymyr the Great, coat of arms of the Principality, two swords, branch of viburnum and wheat.

  • The Odesa region is characterised by a combination of geometric ornaments of different structures and techniques.

    Long sleeves with buttons on the back. Front slit with tassels.

    This was often further emphasised by contrasting colours.

    The surface stitch embroidery technique gives the ornament elements a characteristic elongated shape. Restrained ornaments on such shirts could be complemented by multi-coloured crosses or beads.
